---
title: Clone While It's Hot
date: 2026-03-03
---

Someone said something to me today that I cannot stop thinking about.

Two years ago, Claude existed. The underlying capability was there. But Cursor did not exist yet. Copilot was still finding its footing. If someone had looked at what was clearly working — AI that understands code — and just built a version of it, shipped it, kept iterating, kept productizing — they would be sitting on something real right now.

The lesson is not "be first." The lesson is: **clone what is going viral, then outrun it.**

---

This is not a new idea. It is how most of tech actually works, and we dress it up in language about innovation to avoid admitting it.

WeChat cloned WhatsApp. Instagram cloned features from Snapchat. Google cloned Overture's ad auction model. Every major browser was Netscape with a different team. The clone is almost never the failure mode. The failure mode is cloning and then stopping — thinking the copy is the product.

The window exists every time something goes viral. There is always a period — usually three to six months — where the original is famous but not yet defensible. The team is small. The product is rough. Distribution is thin. The idea has been validated but the execution has not been locked down yet.

That window is the opportunity.

---

The hard part is not building the clone. The hard part is the discipline that comes after.

Ship the clone. Then ship again in two weeks. Then again. New angle, new feature, new insight about who actually needs this and why. Most people who try this approach give up somewhere around week four when nobody has noticed. That is the actual filter. The people who keep going past the point where it feels pointless are the ones who end up with something.

Cursor did not win because they were first or because they were smarter. They won because they had a specific insight — that the whole IDE should be AI-native, not AI-bolted-on — and they kept building that insight into the product long after the obvious version was done.

The clone is just the starting point. You need the angle.

---

Right now, there are things going viral in AI that do not have their definitive version yet.

The category of "AI agent that runs on your desktop and manages your work" — every version of this that exists is half-baked. The category of "AI that knows your codebase, not just your current file" — still not fully solved. The category of "AI that works inside enterprise constraints without external API calls" — almost completely open.

Pick one. Build the obvious version. Then build the non-obvious version. Then keep going.

The window does not stay open forever. But right now, for most of the interesting AI-adjacent categories, it is still open.

---

The people who will look back in two years and say "I should have built that" are building nothing right now. They are watching. Analyzing. Waiting for the right idea.

The right idea is already going viral. They just have not started yet.
